About Us

With alarming rates of population growth in informal settlements vulnerable to climate change, it is imperative to identify risk, advocate change, and implement necessary mitigation and adaptation strategies.

However, climate risk and adaptation research is severely disaggregated because of its global and interdisciplinary nature, which not only complicates data sourcing, but also limits reach of resources. This ultimately deters the timeliness and extensive collaboration required for implementing adaptation strategies, especially in low-income countries where grassroots, locally-led climate resilience is most needed.

So, my project takes on a common data approach to this problem by designing a 'one-stop shop' for climate risk and adaptation research. Similar to an online library, it presents relevant resources to facilitate, streamline, and proliferate advocacy and implementation efforts, as well as foster collaboration between different disciplines and levels. By aggregating, organizing, and extracting data with web scraping and LLM tools, this website can help increase data accessibility and efficiency in the rising field of digital humanitarianism and climate action.
