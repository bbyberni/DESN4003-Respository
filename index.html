About Us

Because of the global and interdisciplinary nature of climate risk and adaptation research, high quality and relevant data in the field can be severely disaggregated and perpetually lack standardization. This not only complicates data sourcing, extraction, and management processes, but also deters the timeliness, collaboration, and stakeholder engagement necessary for local and grassroots efforts in developing countries especially.

So, my project takes on a common data, community-building approach to this problem by designing a social, online repository of open climate resilience resources with AI research assistant tools. This could help facilitate, streamline, and proliferate advocacy and implementation efforts, as well as foster collaboration between different disciplines and levels. By aggregating, organizing, and extracting data with web-scraping and LLM, this website can help increase data accessibility and efficiency in the rising field of digital humanitarianism and climate action, bridging both resource and communication gaps.
